Overview

The UV-Vis Spectrophotometer is a versatile analytical instrument used to measure the absorbance or transmittance of a sample as a function of wavelength in the ultraviolet and visible regions of the electromagnetic spectrum.

Key Specifications

  • Wavelength Range: 190-1100 nm
  • Bandwidth: 1.8 nm
  • Wavelength Accuracy: ±0.5 nm
  • Photometric Range: -4 to 4 Abs
  • Resolution: 0.1 nm
  • Light Source: Deuterium and Tungsten-Halogen lamps

Applications

  • Quantitative analysis of compounds
  • Kinetic studies
  • DNA/RNA/protein quantification
  • Pharmaceutical analysis
  • Environmental monitoring

Overview

The Fourier-Transform Infrared (FT-IR) Spectrometer is used to obtain an infrared spectrum of absorption or emission of a solid, liquid, or gas. It can be used to identify unknown materials, examine the quality of a sample, or study the progress of chemical reactions.

Key Specifications

  • Spectral Range: 7800-350 cm⁻¹
  • Resolution: 0.5 cm⁻¹
  • Beamsplitter: KBr with Ge coating
  • Detector: DLaTGS with KBr window
  • Laser: Helium-Neon laser, 632.8 nm
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio: 40,000:1 (1 minute scan, 4 cm⁻¹ resolution)

Applications

  • Material identification and characterization
  • Polymer analysis
  • Forensic analysis
  • Pharmaceutical quality control
  • Failure analysis

Cost of Analysis

  • For Students: Rs 300 + 18% GST
  • For Academicians: Rs 400 + 18% GST
  • For Industries: Rs 600 + 18% GST

Overview

The Fluorescence Spectrometer is designed for high-sensitivity fluorescence measurements across a wide range of applications. It offers exceptional sensitivity and flexibility for both steady-state and time-resolved fluorescence measurements.

Key Specifications

  • Excitation Range: 250-900 nm
  • Emission Range: 280-900 nm
  • Wavelength Accuracy: ±0.5 nm
  • Resolution: 1.0 nm
  • Detector: R928 Photomultiplier Tube
  • Light Source: 150 W Xenon arc lamp

Applications

  • Fluorescence quantum yield measurements
  • Fluorescence lifetime measurements
  • Protein and nucleic acid analysis
  • Environmental monitoring
  • Pharmaceutical analysis

Overview

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is a technique used to separate, identify, and quantify each component in a mixture. Our system is equipped with multiple detectors for comprehensive analysis.

Key Specifications

  • Pump: Quaternary gradient pump, 0.001-10 mL/min
  • Pressure Range: 0-6000 psi
  • Autosampler: 120-vial capacity, temperature-controlled
  • Column Oven: 5-90°C
  • Detectors: DAD (190-800 nm), FLD, RID, ELSD
  • Injection Volume: 0.1-100 µL

Applications

  • Pharmaceutical analysis
  • Environmental monitoring
  • Food and beverage analysis
  • Forensic analysis
  • Biochemical research
